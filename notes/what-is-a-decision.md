# What is a Decision?

> Draft
>
> Last updated: July 2026

---

## Why this question?

Decision is one of the most fundamental concepts across philosophy, economics, artificial intelligence, neuroscience and biology.

Surprisingly, most definitions assume concepts that are themselves undefined—such as utility, rationality, optimization, preference or intelligence.

The goal of this note is different.

Rather than explaining how decisions should be made, we want to understand the minimum conditions required for a decision to exist.

---

# Current Understanding

> A decision is the selection of one possibility from a set of available alternatives.

This definition intentionally avoids assumptions about:

- intelligence
- rationality
- optimization
- utility
- consciousness

At this stage, none of these concepts appear necessary.

---

# Minimal Conditions

A decision appears to require only four conditions.

## 1. Something is not yet determined.

Before a decision, more than one possible future exists.

---

## 2. Alternatives exist.

Without alternatives, there is nothing to decide.

Execution is not decision.

Reaction is not decision.

Continuation is not decision.

---

## 3. Alternatives are available.

A system cannot choose every possibility that exists.

It can only choose among the alternatives available to it.

Availability may depend on perception, physical capability, internal representation, memory or other constraints.

---

## 4. One alternative is selected.

A decision transforms multiple available possibilities into a single selected possibility.

The action itself may happen later.

---

# Decision Structure

```text
Possibilities

↓

Available Alternatives

↓

Selection

↓

Chosen Possibility

↓

Action (optional)
```

---

# Observations

A decision is not the action itself.

A decision precedes action.

Likewise, a decision is not the representation of the world.

Representation appears to constrain which alternatives become available, but is not itself the decision.

---

# Counterexamples

### A rock rolling downhill

No decision appears to exist.

Why?

Perhaps because there are no alternatives.

---

### A thermostat

Does a thermostat decide?

If so, our definition should explain why.

---

### A bacterium following a chemical gradient

Does the bacterium decide?

Or is it merely reacting?

If this cannot be answered clearly, the current definition is still incomplete.
