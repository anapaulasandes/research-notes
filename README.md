# Research Notes

This repository is a public research notebook. 

These notes exist to explore the question:

> **What are the minimum principles required for intelligent systems to perceive, decide, learn and adapt?**

Definitions are expected to evolve.

---

## Why?

For centuries, decision theory has largely been developed with humans in mind.

Today, we are entering a world populated by increasingly autonomous artificial systems.

Some of these systems already make decisions that affect millions of people.

Future systems may modify their own representations, policies and behaviors in ways we cannot fully predict.

If we wish to understand decision as a fundamental phenomenon, our definitions cannot depend exclusively on consciousness, free will, biology or software implementation.

They should remain meaningful regardless of the substrate on which intelligence emerges.

This repository is an attempt to search for those substrate-independent principles.

---

## Working Principles

* Seek principles before implementations.
* Reduce complexity before adding concepts.
* Prefer questions over conclusions.
* Every definition is provisional.
* Literature should challenge ideas, not replace them.
* A concept is accepted only if it explains more while assuming less.
